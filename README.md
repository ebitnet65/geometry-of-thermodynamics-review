# Geometry of Thermodynamics

A research repository devoted to the geometric foundations of thermodynamics, nonequilibrium statistical mechanics, stochastic thermodynamics, synchronization, and open quantum systems.

## Overview

The central premise of this project is that thermodynamic response is fundamentally geometric. Across classical thermodynamics, stochastic thermodynamics, information geometry, and open quantum systems, physical response can be described in terms of geometric structures such as metrics, connections, curvature, and holonomy.

This repository serves as a working research environment for developing these ideas and collecting the relevant literature, notes, figures, manuscripts, and computational tools.

Current efforts focus on:

- Classical thermodynamic geometry and response theory
- Critical phenomena, fluctuations, and Widom lines
- Stochastic thermodynamics and fluctuation relations
- Synchronization and environment-induced collective order
- Information geometry and quantum Fisher metrics
- Open quantum systems and Liouvillian geometry
- Geometric response and work in nonequilibrium steady states
- Observational thermodynamics and observational holonomy
- Spectroscopic diagnostics of geometric response

---

## Scientific Motivation

Geometry has long played an important role in physics. General relativity describes gravitation through spacetime curvature, gauge theories describe interactions through connections and fibre bundles, and information geometry characterizes statistical distinguishability through metric structures.

Thermodynamics possesses many familiar geometric features, including:

- Work as an area enclosed by a cycle
- Maxwell relations as integrability conditions
- Response functions as local geometric quantities
- Critical phenomena as singular geometric structures

The goal of this project is to develop a unified framework in which thermodynamic response emerges from geometric structures defined on spaces of states, trajectories, controls, and observations.

## Current Major Project

### Response Geometry in Thermodynamics

Invited review article for Physical Chemistry Chemical Physics (PCCP).

Working thesis:

Thermodynamic response is naturally described by geometric structures whose symmetric components encode fluctuations and distinguishability, while antisymmetric components encode cyclic work, irreversibility, and transport.
