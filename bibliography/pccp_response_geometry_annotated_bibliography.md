# Annotated Bibliography for PCCP Review: Response Geometry in Thermodynamics

Working title: **The Geometry of Thermodynamic Response**

This is a first-pass working bibliography organized around the proposed manifesto structure. Entries marked as manuscript, working manuscript, or placeholder should be updated before submission.


## I. Mathematical and geometric foundations

1. **Gibbs1873** — *A Method of Geometrical Representation of the Thermodynamic Properties of Substances by Means of Surfaces*  
   Origin of the geometrical representation of thermodynamic state surfaces; useful as the historical anchor for the manifesto.

2. **Callen1985** — *Thermodynamics and an Introduction to Thermostatistics*  
   Canonical equilibrium thermodynamics reference for potentials, response functions, stability, Maxwell relations, and Jacobian methods.

3. **Needham2021** — *Visual Differential Geometry and Forms: A Mathematical Drama in Five Acts*  
   Pedagogically strong source for differential forms, area laws, orientation, and Stokes-type interpretations used to make the review accessible.

4. **Nakahara2003** — *Geometry, Topology and Physics*  
   Standard physics-facing reference for differential geometry, fiber bundles, connections, curvature, and gauge-theoretic language.

5. **Frankel2011** — *The Geometry of Physics: An Introduction*  
   Broad reference for exterior calculus and geometric structures in physical theories; supports the mathematical language of the review.

6. **AbrahamMarsden1978** — *Foundations of Mechanics*  
   Classical geometric mechanics reference for symplectic manifolds, Hamiltonian flows, and geometric phase concepts.

7. **Arnold1989** — *Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics*  
   Concise reference for symplectic geometry and canonical forms, useful for analogies between mechanics and thermodynamic phase space.

8. **KobayashiNomizu1963** — *Foundations of Differential Geometry, Volume 1*  
   Mathematical reference for connections and curvature; useful if the review formalizes observational holonomy.

9. **Berry1984** — *Quantal Phase Factors Accompanying Adiabatic Changes*  
   The canonical source for geometric phase; conceptually central for any discussion of holonomy and curvature in parameter space.

10. **Simon1983** — *Holonomy, the Quantum Adiabatic Theorem, and Berry's Phase*  
   Provides the holonomy formulation underlying the geometric phase; useful bridge to observational holonomy.

11. **AharonovAnandan1987** — *Phase Change During a Cyclic Quantum Evolution*  
   Extends geometric phase beyond the strict adiabatic limit; relevant when contrasting quasistatic and dynamical geometric response.

12. **WilczekZee1984** — *Appearance of Gauge Structure in Simple Dynamical Systems*  
   Introduces non-Abelian geometric phases; useful for discussing multi-dimensional response spaces and noncommuting observables.


## II. Equilibrium thermodynamic geometry: metric, contact, and symplectic views

6. **Hermann1973** — *Geometry, Physics, and Systems*

   Introduces Legendre-invariant geometrothermodynamics; relevant as a neighboring but distinct program.

8. **Bravetti2015** — *Contact Symmetries and Hamiltonian Thermodynamics*  
   Connects contact Hamiltonian dynamics to thermodynamic transformations; useful bridge from equilibrium geometry to dynamics.

9. **Bravetti2017** — *Contact Hamiltonian Dynamics: The Concept and Its Use*  
   Readable review of contact Hamiltonian dynamics; useful for positioning nonconservative flows.

10. **Grmela2014** — *Contact Geometry of Mesoscopic Thermodynamics and Dynamics*
